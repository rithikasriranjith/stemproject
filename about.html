Introduction

Water quality analysis is the process of measuring physical and chemical characteristics of water to determine whether it is safe for drinking and other purposes. Several important parameters are used to evaluate water quality. The most common parameters include pH level, Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), Turbidity, Dissolved Oxygen, and Temperature.

1. pH Level

The pH level indicates how acidic or alkaline the water is. The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14.

  • pH less than 7 – Acidic water
  • pH equal to 7 – Neutral water
  • pH greater than 7 – Alkaline water

For drinking water, the recommended pH range is between 6.5 and 8.5. If the pH value is below 6.5, the water may become corrosive and harmful to pipes. If the pH is above 8.5, the water may taste bitter and may cause scaling in pipes and appliances.

Minimum Limit: 6.5
Maximum Limit: 8.5

2. Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

TDS represents the total amount of dissolved substances in water such as minerals, salts, metals and organic matter. High TDS levels affect the taste, odor and clarity of water.

Water with very high TDS may not be suitable for drinking and may indicate contamination.

Minimum Limit: 50 mg/L
Maximum Limit: 500 mg/L

3. Turbidity

Turbidity measures the cloudiness or haziness of water caused by suspended particles such as sand, silt, clay or microorganisms.

High turbidity levels may indicate the presence of harmful bacteria, viruses or pollutants.

Clear drinking water should have low turbidity levels.

Minimum Limit: 0 NTU
Maximum Limit: 5 NTU

4. Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

Dissolved oxygen refers to the amount of oxygen present in water. It is important for the survival of aquatic organisms such as fish and plants.

Higher dissolved oxygen levels indicate better water quality. Low oxygen levels may indicate pollution or contamination.

Minimum Limit: 5 mg/L
Maximum Limit: 14 mg/L

5. Temperature

Water temperature affects the chemical reactions and biological activities in water. Higher temperatures reduce the amount of dissolved oxygen and may affect aquatic life.

For drinking water, the temperature should generally be within a moderate range.

Minimum Limit: 10°C
Maximum Limit: 25°C
